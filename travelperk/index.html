Managing conflicting stakeholder visions

TravelPerk / Delta Airlines • 2023-2024

The Challenge

When TravelPerk partnered with Delta Airlines to create an integrated booking experience, we faced a significant challenge: two organizations with very different visions for the product, different timelines, and different definitions of success.

Delta wanted to maintain their premium brand experience and ensure deep integration with their loyalty program. TravelPerk needed to maintain the simplicity and speed that made their platform successful while adding enterprise-grade features that corporate travel managers demanded.

My Role

As Design Lead, I was responsible for navigating these competing priorities while ensuring we delivered a cohesive experience that served both organizations' goals. This meant acting as a bridge between stakeholder groups, translating business requirements into user value, and making tough calls when visions conflicted.

Approach

1. Establishing a shared vision

I started by running collaborative workshops with stakeholders from both organizations. Rather than diving into features, we focused on defining success metrics that both parties could align on: booking conversion rates, user satisfaction scores, and corporate adoption metrics.

2. Creating a decision framework

With competing priorities inevitable, I established a transparent decision-making framework based on user impact and business value. This helped depersonalize difficult conversations and keep the focus on outcomes rather than opinions.

3. Iterative validation

We ran continuous user testing with actual corporate travelers, allowing data to guide decisions when stakeholder opinions diverged. This evidence-based approach helped build trust and align the team around what actually worked.

Impact

The integrated experience launched on schedule and exceeded initial targets, with 40% higher booking conversion than projected. More importantly, we maintained a 4.7/5 user satisfaction score while adding significant complexity to the platform.

The collaboration framework we established became a model for how TravelPerk approached other airline partnerships, reducing integration timelines by an average of 3 months.

Key Learnings

This project reinforced that managing stakeholder alignment is as critical as the design work itself. Success came from establishing shared metrics early, creating transparent decision frameworks, and consistently bringing the conversation back to user value.

I also learned the importance of knowing when to push back and when to compromise. Not every battle is worth fighting, but the ones that directly impact user experience absolutely are.
