Understanding the problem

After the successful launch of Airwallex’s desktop web app, customer research pointed towards the need for a native mobile app. Prior to me joining the team, Airwallex had soft launched an MVP for the app, but the team was uncertain on the direction of the product roadmap.

Applying a customer lens

To understand what out different customer types wanted out of the Airwallex mobile app, I ran a large scale segmented Kano study on potential features that had been proposed by the product team. We surveyed 107 participants for a margin of error of >9% at 95% confidence level.

Challenging our assumptions

The Kano analysis gave us a really clear understanding of the customer sentiment towards our proposed features. Certain features that had been theorised as having a high customer value ranked lower than expected, and other features ranked much higher than initially thought.

Instant access to purchasing

Our first priority was easy access to payment details via biometric validation. We also introduced Apple and Google pay provisioning, which can be triggered from first-time onboarding, and the app home screen.

Reviewing spending limits in real time

By notifying the finance manager as soon as employee’s card is declined due to exceeded spending limits, they’re able to review and change the employee’s limits in real time to prevent important purchases being blocked.

Finance managers’ biggest problem

When surveyed, finance managers said that their biggest problem with managing employee spending is chasing up receipts for expenses. By prompting the employee at the point of sale, the Airwallex app makes it easier and more likely for the employee to quickly snap a photo of their receipt as soon as they make a purchase.

Instant expense approvals

As soon as an employee uploads a receipt and submits an expense request, the finance manager is immediately notified, allowing them to quickly review and approve, wherever they are.

Post-launch

Over six months, I oversaw the reshaping of the product roadmap and 6 subsequent major feature releases that resulted from it. In November 2021, Airwallex announced a further $100million USD of funding, boosting the company valuation to $5.5billion USD.
